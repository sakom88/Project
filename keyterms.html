Key Terms

GPU: The acronym GPU stands for graphics processing unit. Another name for it is the graphics card. It manages how images on your computer are displayed. The power you need from the graphics card will increase as the images become larger and more intricate. Nvidia and AMD are two of the most well-known brands in the field of graphics cards.

CPU: All operations and calculations performed by your computer are carried out by the central processor, also known as the CPU or processor. You can select between AMD and Intel for your PC.

Motherboard: This is where all components are placed that allow them to cooperate and function effectively.

SATA: Similar to USB, SATA is a type of connection that is used to link hard drives and SSDs so that data can be transferred.

PCIe: PCIe is yet another kind of connection, but itâ€™s mostly used with graphics cards as well as M.2 SSDs.

NVMe: The NVMe protocol is a kind of connection protocol that can be used through M.2 SSDs. This gives quick access to saving data and accessing it.

M.2 SSD: A M.2 SSD can be described as a compact stick that supplies your computer with space for storage. You can purchase a SATA-based M.2 SSD or a PCIe-based M.2 SSD, and both support NVMe.

RAM: Data and information processed by the CPU are stored in RAM, also known as random access memory. Your computer will perform its numerous tasks more quickly the more RAM you have and the better CPU you have.

Cooling System: The cooling system is utilized to protect the CPU against overheating.

PSU: The PSU (or power source) powers your PC and the various components it contains with electricity.

OS: OS stands for the operating system. The different operating systems are Windows, Linux, Mac, ios.
