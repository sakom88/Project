Different computer purposes

Everyone uses computers for different things. For some, it’s their primary source of entertainment, in which case you may need to purchase a graphics card and add RAM to enhance your experience. For others, it’s for browsing the web for information or doing a work-related task done traditionally with pen and paper. Then there are the programmers, graphic designers, computer engineers, gamers, etc. These individuals would need to build more robust systems that are versatile and capable of running numerous resource-intensive applications at once!

The type of Computer you need is up to you!

  • If you’re looking for general use, you should budget between $500 and $800.
  • For a moderate user, such as a starter programmer, you should budget around $800-1500.
  • For those looking for the best, then you should be budgeting $2000 and up

Where to buy your computer parts

Buying Computer Parts can get a bit tricky. You must make sure that you’re getting working parts that are backed by warranties. But the first thing you must do is determine what you need your computer to do. Once you have decided that you should consider your CPU and motherboard, we suggest using www.newegg.com, a great resource that will help you match PC component compatibilities. Another great resource is www.pcpartpicker.com

Then we suggest shopping for the best prices for these components in your local stores or online. The safest bet is always to try and purchase named brands, and when doing so, buy it from the manufacturer.

Intel and AMD are the Leaders in the computer chip industry.

There are only two main CPUs on the market, though others are attempting to emerge. But for all your other Computer components, you will need to shop around and find the ones that suit you best.
